Crop growth & development

Checking timing of water usage

The use and availability of water through time is important in driving feedback mechanisms upon crop growth.  The  availability of information quantifying rates of extraction is extremely useful in checking where and when water is extracted.  If data is available the following checking procedures can be followed.

  1. Checking total rate of water extraction.  Compare the predicted timecourse of total soil water in the profile with observations. This can pinpoint error in water stress feedback on crop growth or incorrect root front advance.

  2. Checking the rate of extraction front progress through the profile.  This is driven in the model by crop root front velocity, root hospitality factors and soil moisture content in each layer.  Compare predicted commencement of extraction from each layer with the first observations of extraction in each soil layer.

  3. Checking available water range specification.  The final soil water content may not be the lowest recorded water content through the growing season if infiltration were to exceed extraction late in the crop.  A short study of the observed variation of soil water content through the growing season will test whether this is the case.
