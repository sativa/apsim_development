Soil water balance

Analysing observed data.
Once you have determined that your data exhibits a mass balance you can try and determine just how much extra information can be gleaned from your data.  Look in the following areas:
  • Long periods of dry weather will identify surface air-dry water contents and a description of evaporation timecourse behaviour that can be used to parameterise the evaporation sub-models of a water balance model.
  • Periods following significant rainfall can show both an upper limit of soil water capacity and can show evidence of drainage rates.  Investigation of water content after the drainage events may describe a soil's drained upper limit.
  • Approximate estimates of runoff potential can be calculated from rainfall events onto dry soil for which the wetting front is visible.  (e.g. Runoff = Rain - change in storage to wetting front)
