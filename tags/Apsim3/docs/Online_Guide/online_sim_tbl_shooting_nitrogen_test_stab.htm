Soil nitrogen balance

Test Soil Pool Stability.

Monitoring the temporal variation in soil organic pools can identify instability due to incorrect parameterisation or initial conditions.  Try the following steps to investigate your parameterisation.

  1. Think about what you expect to be happening in your simulation for the soil humic, biomass and fresh organic matter pools.  Take into account the initial state of the soil and the management you are applying during the simulation.  Ask for help if you are unable to determine this yourself.

  2. Output, for each layer, the humic and microbial biomass nitrogen and fresh organic nitrogen and carbon. Consult your module documentation for the names of these output variables. Make sure the output frequency is high enough to capture variation through time.

  3. Plot the output for each layer for each variable and test for sensibility.  The questions you may ask are

    Behaviour Questions

    Increasing through time
    or
    Decreasing through time

    Are the rate and magnitude of the variations reasonable?
    What could be causing these?
    Could any observed asymptote reasonably represent a better estimate of initial conditions?
    If this divergence represents instability, how can it be addressed?

    Oscillations through time

    Are the magnitude and frequency of the oscillation reasonable?
    What could be causing this?
    Does any particular point in the oscillation represent perhaps a better estimate of initial conditions?
