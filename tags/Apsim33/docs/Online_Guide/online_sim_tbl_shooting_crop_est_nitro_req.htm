Crop growth & development

Estimating crop nitrogen requirements

Total Crop Nitrogen requirement will be the sum of the requirements for above ground and below ground growth.

Above Ground

Above ground Nitrogen requirements can be calculated using a Nitrogen Harvest Index (NHI).  This represents the amount of above-ground nitrogen in the yield component at harvest.  Total Nitrogen in the above ground biomass can be calculated from the following

Above Ground N = (Grain N% ÷ 100 ×Yield) / NHI

The following table provides some values of NHI for a range of species.

Crop Species

Nitrogen Harvest Index

Wheat

0.82

Sorghum

0.70

Maize

0.70

For legumes,

above ground N can be calculated from normal crop N concentration as follows

Above Ground N = Above Ground Biomass * N% / 100.

The assumption here is that the N concentration of above ground biomass is approximately 2.0 - 2.5 %.

Below Ground

Below ground Nitrogen requirement is more complex as less is known about this area and the dynamics of growth and die-back are continuous throughout the crop.  An overall estimate of root Nitrogen can be achieved using the following equation

Root N = Above Ground Biomass
            × Root:Shoot ratio of root material
            × C:N ratio of root material
            × Carbon Fraction of Organic Matter (= 0.4)

If in doubt, ask the module support person for estimates of root:shoot and carbon:nitrogen ratios.
