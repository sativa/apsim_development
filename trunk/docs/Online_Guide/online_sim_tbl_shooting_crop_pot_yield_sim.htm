Crop growth & development

Potential yield simulations

Potential yield simulations provide the user with a hypothetical upper limit of yield that can be useful in diverting simulation checking procedures down an appropriate path.  In these test situations water and nitrogen are provided to the crop in excess to crop demand so yield is driven only by radiation, temperature and cultivar data.

How does this help?

  1. If the potential yield is below the measured yield then simply altering resource supply will not help reach the observed numbers.  The validity of the observed data and crop model specification are both called into question.

  2. If the potential yield is above the measured yield the supply of water and Nitrogen can be returned to actual values individually to determine weighting of each factor on growth and pinpoint the areas most likely to assist configuration of the model to reflect the observed crop response.
