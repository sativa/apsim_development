Background Soil Evaporation Theory

All these models are derived from the earliest parametric model of (Black, 1969 - "The prediction of evaporation, drainage and soil water storage for a bare soil", Soil Science society of America Journal 33:655-660) that describes cumulative actual evaporation (Sum(Eact)) during a drying cycle as

Sum(Eact) = a1 t1/2

Where a1 is a parameter describing the evaporation process (mm/day), and t is the time in days after preceding rainfall.

This does not take into account staged evaporation, which ritchie (1972) describes as

    Sum(Eact) = Sum(Epot) for t <= t1 in which Sum(Epot) <= Sum(E1)

    Sum(Eact) = Sum(Epot) + a2 (t-t1)1/2 for t > t1

in which Sum(Epot) is the cumulative potential evaporation during a drying cycle (mm), t1 is the duration of stage 1, Sum(E1) is the cumulative actual evaporation at the end of stage 1, and a2 is a parameter (derived from experimental data?).
