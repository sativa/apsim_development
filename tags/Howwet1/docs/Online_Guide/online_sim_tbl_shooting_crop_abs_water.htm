Crop growth & development

Absence of water information.

The absence of water resource information can significantly limit informed adjustment of simulation configuration.  Some estimate of soil water resource can be derived from the basic system description.  A water balance procedure can be followed using only rainfall and soil water storage information to derive maximum soil water supply.  The upper limit of crop water supply, coupled with some assumptions regarding loss mechanisms can lead the user to some understanding of whether the current simulation parameterisation will be capable of simulating the observed crop.  

For example, a large rainfall event prior to sowing may have filled the soil profile.  This storage plus in-crop rainfall would represent a maximum soil water supply upon which losses mechanisms and crop extraction could operate. (see the section on water balance budgeting form more information).
