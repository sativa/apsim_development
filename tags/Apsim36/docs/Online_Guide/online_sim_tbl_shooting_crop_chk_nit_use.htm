Crop growth & development

Checking overall nitrogen usage

Comparing observed and predicted total Nitrogen uptake can be useful as a first gauge of model behaviour and a pointer to differences in real and virtual descriptions of the simulated system.  

Some things to note:

  1. Check the initialisation values for soil Nitrogen (Check these in the summary file - not the parameter file).  There is little sense in comparing later observations if the initial values are incorrect.

  2. Total Nitrogen uptake in a nitrogen limited crop with minimal starting soil nitrogen is a good estimate of soil mineralisation rates.  Try comparing observed crop nitrogen uptake with predicted nitrogen uptake and predicted nitrogen mineralisation. This comparison will quantify the simulated soil fertility.

  3. Compare observed soil nitrogen uptake by the crop with estimates of crop nitrogen requirement.  Any significant descrepency will help identify areas for further investigation.

  4. Observed soil nitrogen profiles after a crop can tell the user much about rooting patterns and distribution of uptake from the profile.  The main determinants of actual uptake from layers in the model will be time in a layer (due to root advance rates), amount of water extracted from a layer(due to water balance, root advance rates) and overall crop nitrogen demand (due to other nitrogen supplies and overall growth).  See Checking Timing of Nitrogen Usage.
