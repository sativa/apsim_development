Crop growth & development

Step Two - Initial Comparison

Checking Crop Biomass, Nitrogen and Canopy Predictions

Any level of detail of information could exist here but the same principles apply.

  1. It is of little use to reject or accept model behaviour of final crop yield until you have thoroughly checked early growth.

  2. Make sure you are comparing like elements.  For Example:

  • Ensure that predicted green biomass is compared with live material only

  • that units are consistant

  • that plant components compare - measured stem equals predicted stem + leaf sheath (usually the case in field measurement).

Note also that problems in reconciling observed and predicted data may be founded on error in the measured data.  Most errors in observed data should have been removed during the presimulation data analysis and integrity checks.

However remember,

  1. Measurement error is a fact of life.

  2. Cropping area is not homogeneous. Look for variation in replicates in weights, areas, concentrations and planting densities.

  3. Leaf area is related to leaf weight.  The model should get both right or both wrong.

If have identified all variation of simulated from observed data, and you find no grounds for disbelieving observed data at this stage, continue to step three.
