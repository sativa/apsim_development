(This draws from the paper "Simple model for daily evaporation from fallow tilled soil under spring conditions in a temperate climate", J.J.T.I Boesten and L. Stroosnijder, Netherlands Journal of Agricultural Science 34 (1986) 75-90.)

The B&S model

B&S developed a "simple parametric model" to estimate daily evaporation from fallow tilled soil under spring conditions in a temperate climate, in which Epot varies from 1 to 6 mm/day. Their "equation 4" that describes soil evaporation is:

    Sum(Eact) = Sum(Epot) for Sum(Epot) < B2

    Sum(Eact) = Sum(Epot) for Sum(Epot) = Sum(E1) = B2

    Sum(Eact) = B.Sum(Epot)1/2 for Sum(Epot) > B2

in which B (mm1/2) is the evaporation characteristic soil parameter to be determined experimentally. In this equation, Sum(Eact) is not dependant on time.

The two options A and B refer to how the model responds to conditions where daily rainfall excess (rainfall - Epot) is not sufficient to to moisten the dried soil profile to field capacity, ie how the "resets" are implemented.

Option A

Option A assumes that at any time the deficit in soil water throughout the soil profile determines actual evaporation. The procedure is:

On days of no excess rainfall, (rainfall < Epot), Sum(Eact) and also Eact is calculated from the updated value of Sum(Epot) by

    (Sum(Epot))n = (Sum(Epot))n-1+ (Epot - rainfall)n

in which index n is the day number. (Sum(Eact))n is calculated from (Sum(Epot))n by equation 4, and (Eact)n is calculated by

    (Eact)n = (rainfall)n + (Sum(Eact))n - (Sum(Eact))n-1

On days of excess in rainfall (rainfall > Epot),

    (Eact)n = (Epot)n

and the excess of infiltration is subtracted from Sum(Eact):

    (Sum(Eact))n = (Sum(Eact))n-1 - (rainfall - Epot)n

Thereafter, Sum(Epot))n is calculated from Sum(Eact))n with equation 4. If daily rainfall excess greater than Sum(Eact))n-1, then both Sum(Eact))n and Sum(Epot))n are set to zero.

Option B

Option B differs in that Sum(Eact) is always set to zero if there is an excess in rainfall. Calculation of Eact in the subsequent drying cycle is described above by equation 4, up until all water from this rainfall has been evaporated. Then, Sum(Eact) jumps to the value which it had just before the rainfall.

Option B implies that excess in rainfall remains in the upper soil layers at FC and is not redistributed in the drier deeper layers.
