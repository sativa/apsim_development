Crop growth & development

A crop water balance budget.

The underlying principle of a water balance is that the change in soil water status = inputs - losses.

Change in Soil Storage = Irrigation + Rainfall - Runoff - Evaporation - Crop Water Use - Deep Drainage

Conversely,

Crop Water Use = Irrigation + Rainfall - Runoff - Evaporation - Deep Drainage - Change in Soil Water

This bookkeeping relationship can help us check our accounting of water via field measurements.  If the losses are mostly unknown but profile soil water is known we can reformulate the equation again to

Losses = Irrigation + Rainfall  - Change in Soil Water - Crop Water Use.

From this formula we can test that our measured water resource supply would be adequate to allow the measured crop growth (See Estimating Crop Water Use using WUE). 

Losses = Irrigation + Rainfall  - Change in Soil Water - Yield/WUE
where WUE = Water Use Efficiency

Action

If the calculated total losses is insufficient to account for evaporation and runoff estimates (or is even negative)

or

If the calculated losses are too high to be believed then

you may need to revisit observed data or soil specifications.
