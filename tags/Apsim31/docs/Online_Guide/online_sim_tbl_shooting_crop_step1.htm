Crop growth & development

Step One  - Is the simulated phenological development accurate?

Time to anthesis and maturity is an important determinant of crop growth.  It is recommended that simulated anthesis and maturity vary from observed dates by no more than several days.  Errors in the range of a week or two can lead to significant divergence of simulated crop development from observed behaviour.

Things to note:

  1. Anthesis is defined differently for individual species but is often described as the date at which 50% of the heads have flowered.

  2. Anthesis dates are often recorded as "anthesis harvest dates" when crop biomass samples where taken.  The actual crop anthesis date could be significantly prior to this date (eg two weeks) but not often after this date.

  3. Maturity dates are not equal to harvest dates.  Harvest may be some weeks after crop maturity.
