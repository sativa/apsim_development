Crop growth & development

Absence of phenological data.

At this stage you must acknowledge that you do not have information on an important growth determinant.  This does not necessarily stop you from continuing but the error introduced by error in the description of phenological development will always be unknown.

Note:

  1. Farmers usually have some recollection of crop development.  The timing of events can often be linked to the occurrence of other events in the season (eg onset of spring rain). This information gives you some window for a given event.

  2. Field technician diaries might provide clues to events or observation dates that would give a window within which anthesis or maturity may have occurred.
