Soil nitrogen balance

Trouble shooting fallows.
If your simulation contains extended periods with the absence of crops then it should be possible to calibrate the soil fertility status to the observed mineralisation rates.   This procedure is VERY useful and can save many hours of work later on by removing some of the unknowns in the more complicated cropping periods.  The steps to follow are
  1. Set the simulation to run the duration of the experiment but configure the management to initialise the soil water and nitrogen at the beginning of the fallow (ie harvest) to observed data.  It is useful also to initialise the simulation after the fallow as well to constrain cumulative error in the simulation.
  2. Set the simulation to output the soil nitrogen status at the end of each fallow.   Make sure you output both mineral and organic soil nitrogen pools as well as the amount and nitrogen status of surface residues.  It is also useful to have outputs of mineralisation rates throughout the soil profile.
  3. You may decide to minimise error introduced by errors in crop models by removing predicted residues at harvest and manually adding observed residue information when available.  This can be done as part of the crop managment for this phase of testing.
  4. Run the simulation and calibrate the fallow water balance (see section on Trouble shooting fallow water balances).   The feedback of nitrogen/residue dynamics upon the water balance is usually less than the water balance effect on the nitrogen/residue dynamics.  This shall minimise the error in  the water balance upon the mineralisation processes.
  5. Test the Nitrogen and residue dynamics against observed data.

  • Plot nitrogen depth profiles for each sampling period and compare with measured data.

  • Use mineralisation rate information to deduce causes of error.

  • Check the stability of soil organic pools through time
