Crop growth & development

Determining limiting resources

In marginal environments the supply of either water or Nitrogen will become the limiting factor on crop growth.  Similarly, crop growth models reflect the effect of restricted supply of either resource.  It is important to make sure each factor is having an  effect as described by the observed data (as far as the user can deduce).

Deducing limiting factors in observed data

  1. Crops that fail to use all available soil water and yet do not yield highly may be affected by Nitrogen supply or by timing of supply of water.  Check for residual Nitrogen levels in the soil after crop harvest.

  2. Crops that fail to use all available nitrate and yet do not yield highly may be affected by water supply.   Check for residual Nitrogen levels in the soil after crop harvest

  3. Crops that fail to use all available nitrate and yield highly were probably over supplied with Nitrogen and so would be only possibly limited by water supply. A potential yield simulation may prove useful at some stage.

  4. Crops that fail to use all available water and yield well were probably over supplied with water and so would be only possibly limited by Nitrogen supply.  A potential yield simulation may prove useful at some stage.

Deducing limiting factors in predicted data

  1. Ensure that all water and nitrogen stress factors have been selected for output.

  2. Graph factors together through time to see effect of each factor during different stages of growth.
